The both sleepless, alone, slung aslant two beds apart with limbs spread wide, grasping through murk at armfuls of emptiness, at midnight, when dumb thoughts drum anxious and dreams recede to the stiff voice of despair, which whispers dissatisfactions, regrets, failures and disappointment in imprecations for the foibles, the narcissism and the selfishness that waxes with solitude, in the turbid, whirring hollow of a neighbouring pillow, consuming as bodies writhe, as clocks turn, as you lie blind to the dim lucence of deliverance that glisters above, where crowds stare on and I sob, impotent to the reddened tide that climbs ever higher, ever faster, to stain and submerge each floor perched purchase, each saturnine sculpture, whilst you blench and tense and remain, abandoned though not drowned, afloat with the life you cannot live.
