Tearlessly, lovelessly it corrodes, decaying to loose laggards of fingerpressed forearms and newly cut hair, adrift in memory’s cooled current, where longing gazes pale and nervous giggles distort, fading through history’s retreat, relations becoming images and affects attenuating, never to be refreshed, never replenished, a ghostly gift to the rabble that wars with itself, servants that are kings and kings that are servants, thence mouthing truths and lies, gospel and heresy, an uneasy murmur when thinking of her.
